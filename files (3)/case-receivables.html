Receivables Anticipation Platform

Designed a complex financial feature that makes secured receivables (gravame) intuitive and trustworthy for SMB merchants through progressive disclosure.

The Business Context

The receivables anticipation market in Brazil was growing rapidly. 45% of entrepreneurs were already using anticipation products, with competitors like Valorem, Quasar Flash, Adiante, and Weel capturing market share. The Core Challenge: Introduce "gravame" (secured receivables) in a way that merchants with limited financial literacy would understand, trust, and use‚Äîwithout 31% of them being confused about the concept.

Support data showed +11,000 monthly tickets related to anticipation. Analysis revealed 31% of contacts were from users who "didn't know about gravame" or didn't understand the feature. The problem wasn't the product‚Äîit was explaining a complex financial concept to non-financial users.

V1 launched strong with a 90.3 SUM score. Users loved the simplicity and speed. What V1 Learned
  • 80%+ of users wanted same-day settlement when available
  • Component-based tabs worked well but needed better labeling
  • Most users only anticipated occasional values, not full balance
  • Journey was easy and intuitive overall, but gravame terminology confused 31% of first-contact support issues
  • Users needed to understand: What is gravame? Is it risky? Are there hidden fees? Where does money go?
V2 Strategy: Contextual Education

Rather than adding more information upfront, we redesigned the flow to educate users contextually‚Äîat the moment they encounter gravame, not before. The principle: show value first, explain gravame when relevant, build trust through transparency, and emphasize it's exclusive and risk-free.

V2 Testing & Validation
Research Phase

Tested three versions of gravame onboarding in Maze, combined with deep-dive analysis of 70 support contacts. Support data showed: 31% said "I didn't know about gravame," 20% had contestations, 20% didn't receive expected values, 11% had domicile banking issues.

Key Discovery

The support analysis validated our hypothesis: when merchants understood gravame, conversion and satisfaction increased dramatically. The education modal was the solution.

After V2 launch, gravame-related support tickets dropped 31% within 3 months. The 0.15% contact rate meant 99.85% of users completed the feature without contacting support. CSAT of 4.8/5 indicated users felt confident and satisfied. The feature had transformed from confusing to intuitive.

Key Learnings
  • Education Timing Matters: Explaining gravame in context (when encountered) was far more effective than upfront explanation
  • Support Analysis is Gold: Real support data revealed the true pain point‚Äînot usability, but understanding
  • Iteration Drives Improvement: V1 was good (90.3 SUM), but V2 was better because it addressed the actual constraint
  • Progressive Disclosure Works: Users don't need all information at once. Reveal at the right moment
  • Trust Through Transparency: When users see every calculation, every fee, every timeline, confusion becomes confidence
