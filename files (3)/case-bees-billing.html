B2B E-Commerce Billing System

Designed enterprise billing infrastructure for AB InBev's BEES global platform, standardizing manual processes and enabling infinite scalability.

The Enterprise Challenge

BEES‚ÄîAB InBev's B2B e-commerce platform‚Äîwas expanding beyond its core marketplace into services: 1P advertising, premium placements, market intelligence, etc. But billing for these services was entirely manual and inconsistent across zones.

The Problem: Manual invoicing process across zones. Finance teams lacked transparency into fees, costs, and revenue generated from services. No standardization. Zero scalability. Scaling required hiring more people, not automation.

The current process was fragmented: zone finance team ‚Üí category manager ‚Üí get partner data in government system ‚Üí generate NF-S ‚Üí set due dates ‚Üí charge partners. Each step was manual, error-prone, and required cross-functional coordination.

Scalability Requirement

BEES needed a billing system that: (1) centralizes information across zones, (2) integrates with government systems (NF-S), (3) integrates with ServiceNow for ticketing, and (4) enables scaling without proportional headcount increase.

Solution: Centralized & Integrated Platform
01

Single Source of Truth

Centralize all billing data in one system. Zone finance teams enter contracts, service proposals, and payment terms in one place. No more scattered data across zones and spreadsheets.

02

System Integrations

ServiceNow integration for ticketing. Government system integration for NF-S (Nota Fiscal de Servi√ßo) generation. Partner data auto-pulled from government systems. Manual steps reduced from 8+ to 2-3.

03

Automated Charging Cycle

Once configured, the system automatically generates invoices, sends them to partners, and manages charging. Manual work drops ~70%. Billing accuracy increases dramatically.

04

Partner Transparency

Partners can view their charges and understand fee breakdowns. Transparency reduces disputes and support friction. Future: full dispute management system.

Design & Implementation

The system featured three core modules: billing list (all invoices at a glance), invoice creation (3-step flow), and invoice details with multiple states (Draft, Scheduled, Open, Overdue, Paid). Each state provided contextual actions and information.

Key Design Decisions
  • Billing list shows all invoices with filtering by status and date
  • Invoice creation follows a simple 3-step flow: contract/proposal ‚Üí value input ‚Üí summary ‚Üí confirm
  • Invoice details show complete breakdown: what's being charged, when, to which accounts
  • Multiple invoice states (Draft, Scheduled, Open, Overdue, Paid) with distinct visual treatment
  • Future: Support for usage fees, variable costs, integration fees, rebates, and full partner portal

Scalability (vs. Manual)

The centralized billing system achieved 100% process standardization across all zones. What previously required 8+ manual steps per invoice was reduced to 2-3 automated steps. Billing accuracy increased. Cycle time decreased. The organization could now add new services without adding proportional headcount.

The Proof Point: With the manual system, scaling required hiring more finance staff. With the automated system, new services could be added with near-zero incremental labor. The system was infinitely scalable.
Key Learnings
  • Centralization Enables Scale: Enterprise systems fail when trying to accommodate every edge case. By centralizing around a standard process, we created something that scaled.
  • Automation Changes Economics: Reducing 70% of manual work fundamentally changes the operational economics and scalability of the operation.
  • Transparency Reduces Disputes: Partners seeing itemized charges meant fewer disputes and less support overhead. Transparency is a feature.
  • Integration Matters: Rather than building everything from scratch, integrating with ServiceNow and government systems was faster, more reliable, and reduced technical debt.
  • Design for Scalability: When designing enterprise systems, scalability should be a primary constraint, not an afterthought.
